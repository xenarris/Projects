Early History

Most of the early railed transportion had be largely powered by humans, horses, gravity, or stationary engines. It was not until early 19th century when a full scale working steam locomotive was produced. From then on, it was a large contributor to the 2nd industrial revolution and beyond.

Modern day

Nowadays, there are very few steam locomotives or trains. They have been largely replaced by electric, diesel, and gas turbine trains, which are more efficient in nature. Trains are considered the most cost-efficient in cross continental shipments, and is still used extensively today despite the rise in use of other modes of transportation such as cars, planes, and electric boogaloos.

What lies in the Future

As technologies in other sectors of industry mature, they most likely will be incorporated into trains. With many adopting powering things with renewable energy, we can expect to see a growth in electric trains and probably see the eventual technology of plant-powered trains.
